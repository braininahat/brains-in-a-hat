# Grant UI — Design Context

## Users
OSS developers using Claude Code plugins. They stumble on Grant through the marketplace or a recommendation. They want to understand complex systems — codebases, architectures, ML models, algorithms — without reading walls of text. They're technical, impatient, and have high taste.

## Brand Personality
**Clarity. Authority. Craft.**

Grant is the person at the whiteboard who makes everything obvious. Not flashy, not minimal — just precisely right. Every element earns its place.

## Emotional Goals (in priority order)
1. **Clarity** — "Oh, NOW I get it." The fog lifts.
2. **Authority** — "This looks like it came from a published paper." Credible, rigorous.
3. **Wonder** — "Whoa, that's beautiful." Surprising quality.
4. **Speed** — "That was instant." No friction.

## Aesthetic Direction

### References
- **Colah's blog** (colah.github.io) — Hand-crafted SVG/HTML diagrams explaining neural networks. Colorful but purposeful. Spatial layout that builds understanding. The gold standard for technical visualization.
- **Janosh's TikZ repo** (github.com/janosh/tikz) — Publication-quality LaTeX figures of ML architectures. Clean lines, labeled nodes, precise positioning. The authority benchmark.
- **Distill.pub** — Interactive academic figures with scrubbers and hover states. Information density without clutter.

### Anti-References (explicit DO NOTs)
- Mermaid's default blue boxes with sharp corners
- Generic dashboard card grids (the AI slop look)
- Terminal/monospace/hacker aesthetic
- Playful illustrations, mascots, or whimsy
- Dark-mode-with-neon (the "cool" look that says nothing)

### Visual Principles
- **Light backgrounds for diagrams** — like papers and Colah's blog. Dark UI chrome is fine, but the diagram canvas itself should be light for readability and authority.
- **Meaningful color** — color encodes information (type, state, group), never decoration
- **Clean typography** — system-ui or a loaded serif for labels. No monospace except for code.
- **Precise spatial layout** — nodes positioned with intent, not randomly. Alignment matters.
- **Subtle edges** — thin lines, slight curves, tasteful arrowheads. Not thick arrows.
- **Information density** — pack meaning in, but never clutter. Every label earns its place.
- **Rounded but not bubbly** — soft corners (4-6px), not pill shapes or circles everywhere.

## Design Principles

1. **The diagram IS the explanation** — if someone needs to read text to understand the diagram, the diagram failed.
2. **Colah-quality or don't bother** — a bad diagram is worse than no diagram. Only show visuals that genuinely clarify.
3. **Structure reveals meaning** — hierarchy, grouping, flow direction, proximity — all carry information.
4. **Animate to teach, not to decorate** — motion should build understanding step by step, not just look cool.
5. **Respect the viewer's intelligence** — dense, information-rich visuals for technical audiences. Don't oversimplify.
